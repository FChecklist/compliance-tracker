My recommendation: create a new engineering discipline
For VERIDIAN, I'd define a Conversation Execution Engine (CEE) that sits between the LLM and the user. Its mission is to convert structured intents into a consistent conversational experience while minimizing LLM usage. A possible flow is:
Intent Engine – Understand the request.
Workflow Engine – Identify the business process.
Conversation Planner – Decide the next interaction.
Template Library – Reuse predefined messages and prompts.
UI Composer – Prefer forms, buttons, cards, and selectors over free text.
Decision Engine – Apply business rules and deterministic logic.
LLM Gateway – Invoke an LLM only for ambiguity, reasoning, or generation.
Response Renderer – Convert structured results into VERI's conversational style.
Learning Engine – Capture improvements as reusable rules, workflows, or templates instead of expanding future prompts.
With this architecture, the user experiences a natural, human-like conversation with VERI, while the majority of interactions are driven by software. That not only minimizes token consumption but also makes responses faster, more predictable, and easier to govern—qualities that are especially valuable in enterprise ERP systems.
